Introduction

This documentation provides a summary of a re-run of experiment run 58 performed at the University of Minnesota (UMN). The main goal of the run was to use the 60keV line to measure the charge collection efficiency. We also had additional goals of determining the maximum bias voltage before breakdown and neutralizing the detector under bias after a certain duration.

Dr. Mei and his team grew the germanium crystal, “USD1,” at the University of South Dakota. Then, a team at Texas A&M polished and fabricated the crystal with ionization readout channels. Finally, the detector was wire bonded, mounted in a dilution fridge and tested at UMN.

Detector and Sources

We tested a germanium detector that is 32mm thick and 100mm in diameter. We used four Americium-241 (Am-241) sources mounted on top of each channels on the detector (see Figure 1). The pinholes allow 60keV gammas, along with alphas, through to the detectors.

Figure 1: Shows the orientation of the 4 Am-241 source plates before it was installed on the detector

 

We observed 60keV peaks in spectra from each channel and performed different measurements over a course of two-weeks. We used the data masured at various bias voltages to characterize the charge collection efficiency as a function of bias for each channel.
